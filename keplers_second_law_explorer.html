Position and velocity are computed by solving Kepler's equation directly (Newton's method) at every step — not approximated. The gravity vector shown is acceleration, GM/r² (force per unit mass), since no planet mass is assumed. Vector lengths are compressed (√) so both near- and far-orbit values stay visible on screen; their relative lengths still grow and shrink correctly with r. Swept areas are computed by direct numerical integration of the actual path (summing triangles from the Sun), not assumed equal from the law.
