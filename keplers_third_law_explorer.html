Orbits are treated as circular at each planet's semi-major axis, since real eccentricities are too small to see at this scale (except Mercury & Mars, which are slightly exaggerated visually here). In AU/year units, Kepler's constant is 1, so the T² vs a³ line has slope 1 — that's k for our solar system.
